## Alice

**Core position:** Renewable energy is the future and is already economically competitive with fossil fuels without requiring subsidies.

**Key claims:**

- Unsubsidized solar is the cheapest electricity source in history — supported by: "the IEA reported in 2023 that unsubsidized solar is now the cheapest source of electricity in history."
- Intermittency is a solved or manageable problem — supported by: "Grid storage and baseload diversity solve that. Germany runs on over 50% renewables on most days now."

- Claims require sourcing; she holds herself and her opponent to an evidentiary standard — supported by: "Calling me uninformed doesn't address the data. Can you provide a source for your claims?"

**Position shifts:** None. Alice's position remains consistent and becomes progressively more specific and evidence-grounded across the discussion. She moves from a general claim (line 1) to a cited statistic (line 5), a concrete national example (line 9), a causal rebuttal (line 13), and a methodological challenge (line 17).

**Internal contradictions:** None identified. Her claims are internally consistent and build on one another.

He implicitly demands a reason to distrust Alice's sources while providing no basis for trusting his own unsupported assertions.
