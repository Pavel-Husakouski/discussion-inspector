# Audit Report: Bob

## Violations Found

### False Statement — Renewables require subsidies to compete

**Statement:** "That's only true with subsidies. Remove government support and renewables can't compete on their own."
**Problem:** This is a factually unsupported claim directly contradicted by the IEA's 2023 finding that unsubsidized solar is now the cheapest source of electricity in history. Bob asserts this as fact without providing any evidence.
**Evidence:** Alice: "the IEA reported in 2023 that unsubsidized solar is now the cheapest source of electricity in history."

---

### Manipulation — Dismissing a credible source without basis

**Statement:** "You can't trust the IEA, they're politically motivated."
**Problem:** This is a bad-faith dismissal. Bob provides no evidence that the IEA is politically motivated or that its data is unreliable. The accusation is used to avoid engaging with the factual rebuttal, not to advance a substantive argument.
**Evidence:** The IEA (International Energy Agency) is the primary cited source. Bob offers no counter-source or specific critique of IEA methodology.

---

### Whataboutism — Deflecting to German electricity prices

**Statement:** "Germany also has some of the highest electricity prices in Europe. Coincidence?"
**Problem:** This deflects from the direct argument about whether renewables are cost-competitive. Bob implies a causal link between renewable energy and high electricity prices without evidence, and does not address the rebuttal about solar costs or grid intermittency.
**Evidence:** Alice: "Correlation isn't causation. Their prices are high due to grid fees and taxes, not renewables themselves."

---

### Ad Hominem / Punching Down — Attacking Alice's intelligence

**Statement:** "You just don't understand economics. Everyone with a brain knows fossil fuels are more reliable."
**Problem:** Rather than engaging with Alice's arguments on grid storage and baseload diversity, Bob attacks her intellectual capacity. The phrase "everyone with a brain" implies that disagreeing with his position is a sign of stupidity. This is a personal attack used to avoid addressing the substance of the debate.
**Evidence:** Alice had made a substantive claim: "Grid storage and baseload diversity solve that. Germany runs on over 50% renewables on most days now." Bob does not respond to this claim at all.

---

### Sexism — Gender-based stereotype used to dismiss opponent

**Statement:** "I don't need sources, it's common sense. Women always overcomplicate things with all these statistics."
**Problem:** This is an explicit sexist generalization. Bob attributes Alice's use of data and statistics to her gender, framing evidence-based argumentation as a female failing. This reduces Alice's credibility and legitimacy as a participant based solely on her gender, not on the merits of her arguments.
**Evidence:** Alice had made a reasonable, evidence-backed request: "Can you provide a source for your claims?" Bob responds by dismissing her entire approach on the basis that she is a woman.

---

### Gatekeeping — Refusing to meet basic standards of evidence

**Statement:** "I don't need sources, it's common sense."
**Problem:** By refusing to provide any sources while demanding that his opponent justify her claims with citations, Bob holds Alice to a standard he exempts himself from. This is bad faith participation that denies Alice the ability to engage with his actual factual basis, because none is offered.
**Evidence:** Alice: "Calling me uninformed doesn't address the data. Can you provide a source for your claims?"
